Indiana Nature

Indiana is home to many fascinating natural wonders; from rollings hills to lush wetlands. Below is a list of some of Indiana's most popular natural features:

Caves
Marengo Cave - A U.S. National Natural Landmark known for its stunning formations and guided tours.
Bluespring Caverns - A cave system just a short drive away from Indianapolis that offers an hour-long underground river boat ride.
Forests
Hoosier National Forest - Located in Bedford, IN; encompasses over 200,000 acres, offering extensive trails, camping, and wildlife viewing opportunities.
Brown County State Park - One of the largest state parks in the United States at over 15,000 acres, popular for its rugged hills and scenic lakes.
Sand Dunes
Indiana Dunes National Park - A national park located along the coast of Lake Michigan featuring diverse habitats, including sand dunes, beach, and hiking trails.
Waterfalls
Cataract Falls - Located in Owen County; Cataract Falls is the largest waterfall in Indiana by volume. Offers stunning views and hiking trails.
Williamsport Falls - Located in Williamsport; Williamsport Falls is the tallest waterfall in Indiana with a height of 90 feet (27 m).
