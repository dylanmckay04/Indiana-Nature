Indiana Wildlife

A wide variety of wildlife can be found in Indiana, including many native species of birds, mammals, fish, and insects. Here is some information about different species of animals you may find:

Wildlife Species in Indiana
Common Name Scientific Name Habitat Conservation Status Notes
Indiana Bat Myotis sodalis Forested Areas Endangered Known for hibernating in cold caves during winter.
Sandhill Crane Antigone canadensis Wetlands Stable Migratory birds, seen most during spring and fall migrations.
Eastern Box Turtle Terrapene carolina carolina Woodlands Vulnerable Has a high dome-like shell and prefers moist forest floors.
Red Fox Vulpes vulpes Forests, grasslands Stable Highly adaptable and can live in close proximity to urban areas.
White-tailed Deer Odocoileus virginianus Forests, fields Stable Common throughout the state, seen the most around dawn and dusk.
